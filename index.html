About Formula 1

Formula 1 (F1) is the highest level of international single-seater automobile racing, governed by the FIA. It represents the pinnacle of motorsport technology, combining elite driver skill with cutting-edge engineering. Each season consists of a series of races called Grands Prix, held worldwide on purpose-built circuits and street tracks.

Modern F1 cars are powered by hybrid power units combining turbocharged V6 engines with advanced energy recovery systems. They can reach speeds over 350 km/h and generate immense aerodynamic downforce, allowing them to corner at extraordinary speeds.

Formula 1 is not just about speed — it is a global spectacle involving strategy, tire management, pit stops, team coordination, and constant technological innovation. With millions of fans worldwide, F1 continues to push the boundaries of performance and competition.
